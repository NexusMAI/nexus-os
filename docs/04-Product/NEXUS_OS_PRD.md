# 1. Document Control

## Purpose

This Product Requirements Document defines the product requirements for Nexus OS.

It serves as the authoritative specification describing what the platform must accomplish, why it exists, who it serves, and the capabilities required for implementation.

This PRD is governed by:

- Nexus Vision
- Nexus Architecture
- Nexus Engineering Workflow
- Nexus Engineering Constitution

In the event of conflicts, the Engineering Constitution takes precedence.

---

# 2. Executive Summary

Nexus OS is an AI-First Engineering Operating System designed to standardize the complete lifecycle of software engineering.

Rather than functioning as a software framework, Nexus OS provides a reusable engineering platform that combines:

- Engineering methodology
- AI orchestration
- Knowledge management
- Evaluation
- Governance
- Templates
- Standards
- Automation

Its objective is to enable teams to consistently produce production-ready software through structured collaboration between humans and AI agents.

Every future Nexus project—including Nexus μAI—will inherit this operating model.

---

# 3. Problem Statement

Modern AI-assisted software development faces several systemic problems:

## Engineering Problems

- Inconsistent development processes
- Poor documentation quality
- AI-generated code without verification
- Knowledge fragmentation
- Reinventing architectures
- Lack of reusable engineering assets
- No standardized evaluation methodology
- Weak engineering governance
- Difficulty scaling AI collaboration

## Organizational Problems

Engineering quality varies significantly between projects.

Each project often invents its own workflow, architecture, templates, prompts, and documentation.

This results in:

- duplicated work
- inconsistent quality
- reduced maintainability
- increased engineering cost
- slower product delivery

Nexus OS exists to solve these problems through standardization.

---

# 4. Strategic Context

Software engineering is rapidly evolving toward AI-assisted development.

Future engineering organizations will coordinate work between:

- Human Engineers
- AI Architects
- AI Developers
- AI Reviewers
- AI Researchers
- AI Evaluators

Traditional SDLC processes do not provide sufficient governance for this model.

Nexus OS establishes a unified operating model that enables AI-first engineering while preserving human accountability.

The platform is designed to evolve alongside advances in AI rather than being tied to any individual model or vendor.

---

# 5. Product Vision & North Star

## Vision

To become the engineering operating system that enables humans and AI agents to collaboratively design, build, evaluate, and continuously improve production-grade software.

## North Star

Every project built using Nexus OS should demonstrate:

- consistent architecture
- repeatable workflows
- measurable quality
- reusable knowledge
- auditable decisions
- production readiness

## Long-Term Vision

Nexus OS will evolve into a reusable engineering platform capable of supporting multiple domains including:

- Medical Robotics
- Computer Vision
- AI Research
- Full Stack Engineering
- Data Platforms
- Enterprise AI Systems

without changing its engineering methodology.

---

# 6. Goals & Non-Goals

## Goals

Nexus OS MUST provide:

- standardized engineering workflows
- reusable project templates
- AI agent orchestration
- prompt management
- engineering governance
- evaluation pipelines
- documentation standards
- knowledge management
- architecture standards
- quality gates
- automation support

Every project MUST inherit these capabilities.

## Non-Goals

Nexus OS does NOT aim to:

- replace software engineers
- replace product management
- become an application framework
- provide domain-specific business logic
- become a low-code platform
- function as an IDE
- replace Git or GitHub
- lock users into any AI provider

The platform defines *how* engineering occurs, not *what* individual products do.

# 19. System Architecture (Conceptual)

Nexus OS consists of modular engineering subsystems working together as an engineering operating system.

Core subsystems include:

- Workflow Kernel
- Execution Runtime
- Context Manager
- Knowledge Base
- Capability Registry
- Evaluation Engine
- Artifact Repository
- Policy & Access Control
- Scheduler & Orchestrator
- Observability & Audit System

Each subsystem exposes clear responsibilities, interfaces, and governance boundaries.

Business logic remains external to the platform.

Nexus OS coordinates engineering—not application execution.

# 21. Security, Compliance & Governance

Security is a platform-wide responsibility.

Every engineering artifact MUST comply with constitutional governance.

Platform requirements include:

- Role-based access control
- Audit logging
- Version history
- Secret management
- Least privilege
- Approval workflows
- Policy enforcement

Sensitive engineering assets MUST be protected throughout their lifecycle.

Governance applies equally to humans and AI agents.

---

# 22. AI Safety & Reliability Requirements

AI systems operating within Nexus OS SHALL satisfy defined safety requirements.

Requirements include:

- Human approval for critical actions
- Explainable engineering decisions
- Prompt versioning
- Output traceability
- Hallucination mitigation
- Evaluation before deployment
- Controlled autonomy
- Failure escalation

AI agents MUST never self-authorize production deployment.

Safety requirements override automation objectives.
